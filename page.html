    • 5 Guidelines for dealing with the press

  • Content must be topical and newsworthy
  • Media reports must always be factual, obective and concise
  • Press release must not be longer than 300 words
  • Provide photographs where possible
  • Never tell journalist something in confidence

    • What does the structure of a press release consist of?

  • Date
  • Clear heading & headlines
  • Use present tense
  • Type "END" at the end of press release
  • 200-300 words, double line-spaced
  • Provide all contact details

    • 5 Types of organisational news that can be included in a house journal

  • New products, services and policies
  • Community projects, advertising campaigns & sponsorships
  • Introduce new employees, promotions, transfers
  • Information on incentive schemes, safety and health guidelines
  • Simple descriptions of procedures concering business or factory

    • 5 Requirements of Brochures

  • Be brief, concice & cover only one topic
  • Information must be easy to understand,
  • Wide margins & enough white spaces: make it easy to read
  • Use the correct size: A4-size
  • Use suitable & meaningful photographs of people or activities
  • Use the AIDCA

    • What are the key elements in an annual report?

  • Chairperson/CEO report
  • Operating review
  • Profile of board of directors & executives committee
  • Financial statements
  • Highlights of financial summary of year
  • Overview of business & social-welfare activities
